## The staff is a pitch grid

A staff has five lines and four spaces. Notes placed higher on the staff usually sound higher. Notes placed lower usually sound lower.

<NotationFigure title="Four rising notes on a treble staff" notation="C4/q, D4, E4, F4" />

## Clefs give the grid a starting point

A clef tells you which pitch area the staff is showing. Treble clef is common for higher notes. Bass clef is common for lower notes. The symbol changes how a line or space is named.

<NotationFigure title="A lower register in bass clef" notation="C3/q, D3, E3, F3" clef="bass" />

<Callout title="Do not memorize every note at once" tone="melody">
  Use a few anchors first. Middle C, the treble spaces, and the bass landmarks will make the staff less crowded.
</Callout>

## Ledger lines extend the staff

When a note moves beyond the five-line staff, short ledger lines continue the same pattern. They are not a new system. They are extra steps.
