## Scales choose notes from the octave

A scale is a selected path through the octave. The major scale uses this whole-step and half-step pattern:

**whole, whole, half, whole, whole, whole, half**

## Scale degrees name the positions

Scale degrees number the notes inside a scale. In C major, C is 1, D is 2, E is 3, and the pattern continues to B as 7.

## Minor scales are a family

Natural minor, harmonic minor, and melodic minor share a minor center but adjust upper scale degrees differently. This course introduces the family now and saves detailed minor writing for the next release.

## Key signatures reduce repeated accidentals

A key signature places sharps or flats at the start of a staff so the music does not need to rewrite them each time. The circle of fifths organizes these signatures by adding or removing one sharp or flat at a time.
