## What pitch names do

Music theory starts by giving repeatable names to sound. A pitch is a sound you can place higher or lower than another sound. In this course, pitch names use seven letters:

**A B C D E F G**

After G, the letters loop back to A. The loop is useful because the next C sounds closely related to the C before it, only higher or lower.

## Octaves keep the loop organized

An octave is the span from one note letter to the next version of the same letter. The octave number tells you the register. C4 and C5 share the letter C, but C5 is higher.

<Callout title="First anchor" tone="melody">
  When a label has a letter and a number, read the letter first and the register second. C4 means the note C in octave 4.
</Callout>

## Natural notes

The white keys on a piano show the seven natural note letters. They are not evenly shaped on the keyboard, but the letter order stays steady.
