## Half steps and whole steps

A half step is the distance from one piano key to the very next key, black or white. A whole step is two half steps.

<KeyboardFigure label="Chromatic keyboard area" active={["C", "C#", "D", "D#", "E"]} />

## Accidentals alter a note

A sharp raises a note by a half step. A flat lowers a note by a half step. A natural cancels a previous sharp or flat in the current context.

## Enharmonic spellings

Two names can point to the same piano key. C# and Db are common enharmonic partners. The spelling matters because music notation also tracks letter movement and harmonic meaning.

<Callout title="Spelling is information" tone="melody">
  If two notes sound alike on the keyboard, do not assume they mean the same thing in notation. The letter name can explain the musical job.
</Callout>
