## Intervals measure distance

An interval is the distance between two notes. First count the letter names for the generic size: C to E is a third because C, D, E spans three letters.

<AudioExample label="C up to E" notes={["C4", "E4"]} />

## Quality gives the exact size

Interval quality adds precision. C to E is a major third. C to Eb is a minor third. The letter distance is the same, but the half-step size changes.

<NotationFigure title="Major third and minor third" notation="C4/q, E4, C4, Eb4" />

## Melodic and harmonic intervals

A melodic interval is heard one note after the other. A harmonic interval is heard at the same time.

<AudioExample label="Harmonic major third" notes={["C4", "E4"]} mode="chord" />

## Inversion flips the notes

When the lower note moves above the higher note, the interval is inverted. C up to E is a third. E up to C is a sixth.
