## Beat is the steady layer

The beat is the pulse you can tap. Rhythm is the pattern of sound and silence that happens against that pulse. A rhythm can be busy while the beat stays even.

<AudioExample label="Four steady beats" notes={["C4", "C4", "C4", "C4"]} />

## Measures group the pulse

A measure is a small container for beats. A time signature tells you how the beat is grouped. In 4/4, four quarter-note beats fill one measure.

<NotationFigure title="One measure of four quarter notes" notation="C4/q, C4, C4, C4" />

## Duration marks

Notes show sound duration. Rests show silence duration. Dots lengthen a value. Ties connect written notes into one held sound. Triplets divide a beat into three even parts.

Use this small map when the symbols start to blur:

- **Note value:** how long a written sound lasts.
- **Rest:** a measured silence that still counts inside the beat.
- **Dot:** an added half of the note or rest value before it.
- **Tie:** two written notes connected into one sustained sound.
- **Triplet:** three equal parts placed where the beat would normally divide into two.

<Callout title="Rhythm reading tip" tone="rhythm">
  Count the container before naming every symbol. Knowing the measure size makes each duration easier to place.
</Callout>
