## A blues is a twelve-bar shape

The twelve-bar blues is one of the most reused forms in popular music. It uses just three chords — I, IV, and V — over twelve bars, usually as dominant sevenths for the bluesy color.

<AudioExample label="C7 dominant color" notes={["C4", "E4", "G4", "Bb4"]} mode="chord" />

## The standard layout

Bars follow this map in C: I I I I, IV IV I I, V IV I V. The final V is the "turnaround" that pushes back to the top.
