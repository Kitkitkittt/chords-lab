## A small song can be built from blocks

Start with a beat pattern, then add a bass note, a chord, and a short melody. Repetition is useful: it lets the listener understand the loop before anything changes.

<Callout title="Theory becomes a sketch" tone="harmony">
  A Roman numeral can choose the chord block. A scale can choose the melody notes. Rhythm decides where the notes land.
</Callout>

## Form is the order of sections

A simple A A B A plan repeats one idea, visits a contrasting idea, then returns. The Song Lab uses generated patterns so learners can explore without needing copied songs.
