## A triad stacks three chord tones

A triad is a three-note chord built from a root, a third, and a fifth. The root names the chord. The third and fifth show its quality.

## Four beginner triad qualities

Major triads sound like a major third plus a perfect fifth from the root. Minor triads lower the third. Diminished triads lower the third and fifth. Augmented triads raise the fifth.

## Inversions move the bass

Root position has the root as the lowest note. First inversion places the third in the bass. Second inversion places the fifth in the bass.

<Callout title="Chord symbols are compact labels" tone="harmony">
  C means C major in many lead-sheet contexts. Cm means C minor. Cdim and Caug add the altered fifth quality to the label.
</Callout>
